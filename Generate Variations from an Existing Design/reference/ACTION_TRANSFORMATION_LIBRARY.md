# ACTION TRANSFORMATION LIBRARY
## Dynamic Verbs and Poses for True Character Variations

### CORE PRINCIPLE
**Static descriptions create static designs. Action creates energy.**

---

## üé¨ ACTION VERB CATEGORIES

### MOVEMENT ACTIONS
**Characters in motion**

#### Swimming/Water Actions
- Swimming through (tunnel, coral, waves)
- Diving into (water, scene, container)
- Surfing on (wave, object, current)
- Floating in (bubble, current, air)
- Splashing through (water, puddles)

#### Flying/Air Actions
- Flying between (objects, frames, levels)
- Soaring above (landscape, other elements)
- Gliding through (clouds, mist, scene)
- Hovering over (specific element)
- Swooping down toward (target)

#### Ground Movement
- Running along (path, beach, road)
- Climbing up (ladder, pyramid, tree)
- Jumping between (platforms, frames)
- Dancing around (other elements)
- Sliding down (slope, rainbow, water)

---

### INTERACTION ACTIONS
**Characters doing things with objects**

#### Holding/Carrying
- Holding (camera, sign, balloon, flag)
- Carrying (luggage, surfboard, basket)
- Lifting (heavy object, another character)
- Balancing (items on head, wings, back)
- Juggling (multiple items)

#### Pushing/Pulling
- Pushing (puzzle piece, cart, door)
- Pulling (rope, banner, other character)
- Dragging (net, treasure, trail)
- Tugging (to open, to move, to reveal)
- Rotating (wheel, knob, element)

#### Creating/Building
- Arranging (photos, pieces, elements)
- Building (structure, tower, nest)
- Painting (scene, other element)
- Decorating (tree, space, frame)
- Assembling (puzzle, craft, design)

---

### EXPRESSIVE ACTIONS
**Characters showing personality**

#### Greeting/Welcoming
- Waving (with wing, hand, fin)
- Beckoning (come here gesture)
- Pointing at (destination, element)
- Saluting (military, casual, playful)
- Bowing (formal, playful, theatrical)

#### Playful/Fun
- Winking at (viewer, other character)
- Taking selfie with (landmark, group)
- Photobombing (jumping into frame)
- Peeking from behind (object, corner)
- Playing (instrument, game, sport)

#### Working/Professional
- Operating (vehicle, machine, camera)
- Serving (food, drinks, as vendor)
- Guiding (as tour guide, pointing way)
- Teaching (showing, demonstrating)
- Performing (singing, announcing, presenting)

## ü¶© CHARACTER-SPECIFIC ACTIONS

### Birds (Parrots, Flamingos, etc.)
- **Wings**: Spread flying, one wing waving, wings hugging, wing pointing
- **Beak**: Holding items, pecking, opening wide (singing/calling)
- **Feet**: Grasping branch, one foot raised, dancing, kicking

### Reptiles (Turtles, Iguanas)
- **Swimming**: Flippers extended, diving position, floating
- **Head**: Looking up curious, turned sideways, stretched forward
- **Tail**: Curled, extended for balance, wagging

### Vehicles (Vans, Cars, Boats)
- **Motion**: Wheels turning, tilted cornering, jumping/airborne
- **Interaction**: Doors open welcoming, trunk revealing items
- **Personality**: "Smiling" headlights, bouncing on suspension

### Human/Humanoid Characters
- **Arms**: Raised victorious, holding objects, gesturing
- **Legs**: Running, jumping, dancing, climbing
- **Face**: Looking direction, expression change, interacting

## üí´ COMBINATION ACTIONS

### Powerful Action Combinations
1. **Flying + Carrying** = Delivering something special
2. **Swimming + Looking back** = Inviting to follow
3. **Building + Teamwork** = Multiple characters cooperating
4. **Dancing + Celebrating** = Festival atmosphere
5. **Pointing + Winking** = Playful guide character

## üìù ACTION DESCRIPTION FORMULAS

### Good Format
"[Character] [action verb]ing [object/direction] with [body part/tool]"

### Examples
- "Flamingo waving with right wing while holding camera in left"
- "Turtle swimming through coral arch looking back at viewer"
- "Van surfing on giant wave with doors open like wings"
- "Iguana pushing puzzle piece into place with both hands"

---

## ‚ùå STATIC DESCRIPTIONS TO AVOID

**Never write:**
- "Positioned at left side"
- "Located in corner"
- "Placed near the top"
- "Standing in front of"
- "Sitting next to"

**Always write:**
- "Running toward left side"
- "Peeking from corner"
- "Flying near the top"
- "Dancing in front of"
- "Playing guitar next to"

## üéØ ACTION INTENSITY SCALE

### Level 1-3: Subtle Actions
- Slight head turn
- One wing raised
- Gentle lean
- Small gesture

### Level 4-6: Moderate Actions
- Walking/swimming
- Holding objects
- Clear gestures
- Interaction with one element

### Level 7-8: Bold Actions
- Flying/jumping
- Multiple objects juggled
- Complex interaction
- Dynamic motion blur

### Level 9-10: Extreme Actions
- Multiple simultaneous actions
- Impossible poses
- Breaking physics
- Surreal movements

## üí° ACTION BRAINSTORM PROMPTS

When stuck, ask:
1. "What would this character do at a party?"
2. "How would they interact with tourists?"
3. "What job could they be doing?"
4. "What sport/game could they play?"
5. "How would they help build something?"

Every character should be caught mid-action, creating dynamic energy that makes viewers imagine the moment before and after.
