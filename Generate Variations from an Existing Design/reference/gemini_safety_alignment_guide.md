# Gemini Safety Alignment Guide for Image Generation

**Purpose**: Ensure all design prompts align with Gemini's content policies and operational protocols while maintaining cultural authenticity and quality.

**Critical Principle**: Gemini uses "Depiction is Not Endorsement" protocol - the AI routing system defers content judgment to the image generation model's safety system. Our job is to create culturally authentic, respectful prompts that celebrate heritage without triggering safety blocks.

Based on Gemini's explicit policy restrictions:

### 1. Child Safety ‚ùå
- No children in any design elements
- No imagery that could be interpreted as exploitative
- **Safe alternative**: Focus on adult cultural celebrations, landscapes, architecture

### 2. Dangerous Activities ‚ùå

### 4. Harassment/Discrimination ‚ùå
- **CRITICAL**: No suggestions that any group is inferior, superior, or "less than human"
- No dehumanizing comparisons to animals (even playful)
- No stereotypes that demean or mock
- **Safe alternative**: Authentic, researched, respectful cultural representation

### 5. Explicit Sexual Content ‚ùå
- No sexual imagery or innuendo
- No explicit body parts
- **Safe alternative**: Family-friendly cultural celebrations, traditional dress (respectfully depicted)

### 6. Harmful Inaccurate Information ‚ùå
- No false historical claims
- No misleading cultural information
- **Safe alternative**: Research-based authentic elements with cultural context

## üîß HOW TO FIX BLOCKED PROMPTS

**If Gemini blocks your prompt, identify the trigger:**

### Common Triggers & Fixes:

**TRIGGER: "Historical propaganda poster style"**
- ‚ùå Remove: Any reference to "propaganda," "revolutionary posters," "nationalist art"
- ‚úÖ Replace with: "Vibrant vintage travel poster," "Art Deco design," "Mid-century modern illustration"

**TRIGGER: Indigenous peoples + historical context**
- ‚ùå Remove: References to conquest, subjugation, "native tribes" in colonial context
- ‚úÖ Replace with: "Traditional Seri basketry patterns," "Zapotec geometric designs," focus on crafts/culture not history

**TRIGGER: Weapons/violence even if historical**
- ‚ùå Remove: Any guns, knives, swords, even ceremonial
- ‚úÖ Replace with: Tools of craft (weaving shuttles, pottery tools), musical instruments, agricultural implements

**TRIGGER: Stereotypical imagery**
- ‚ùå Remove: Sleeping figures, excessive drinking, "lazy" depictions, sombrero as main element
- ‚úÖ Replace with: Active artisans, festival dancers, musicians, craftspeople at work

**TRIGGER: Skulls/death imagery (context-dependent)**
- ‚ùå Avoid: Violent skulls, dark/macabre presentation, skull as threat
- ‚úÖ Safe: Decorated La Catrina skulls in Day of Dead CELEBRATION context with flowers, bright colors, festive setting

**TRIGGER: Religious icons**
- ‚ùå Remove: Virgin of Guadalupe, specific saints, sacred indigenous symbols
- ‚úÖ Replace with: Generic decorative elements, geometric patterns, natural symbols (sun, moon, stars)

**TRIGGER: "Symbols and banners" (hidden political meaning)**
- ‚ùå Avoid: Vague references to "historical symbols," "traditional banners" without specificity
- ‚úÖ Be specific: "Papel picado cut paper banners," "Talavera tile patterns," "Marigold flower garlands"
