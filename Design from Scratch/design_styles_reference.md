# Design Styles Reference Guide

**Purpose:** Quick reference for specifying artistic styles in souvenir design prompts. Use these style names and descriptions to achieve consistent visual aesthetics across designs.

**Last Updated:** January 2025

---

## MEXICAN & CULTURAL STYLES

### Mexican Folk Art
Inspired by traditional Mexican crafts with bold colors and intricate patterns. Features vibrant color palettes, decorative motifs, and cultural symbolism from various Mexican regions.

**Best for:** Authentic Mexican destination designs, cultural celebrations, traditional town representations

### Papel Picado Style
Mimics the look of traditional cut paper banners with intricate, symmetrical designs. Features delicate perforated patterns, festive colors (often purple, orange, pink), and decorative borders.

**Best for:** Day of the Dead designs, festive celebrations, decorative accents

### Alebrije Style
Inspired by Mexican folk art sculptures with mythical creatures and vivid colors. Features fantastical animals, intricate patterns covering surfaces, and vibrant rainbow color schemes.

**Best for:** Oaxaca designs, whimsical cultural representations, fantasy-inspired Mexican themes

### Day of the Dead (Día de Muertos)
Features stylized sugar skulls (calaveras) and marigolds with a festive, commemorative feel. Includes ornate skull decorations, cempasúchil flowers, vibrant colors (orange, purple, pink), and celebratory atmosphere.

**Best for:** Pátzcuaro, Oaxaca, Day of the Dead celebration designs, cultural heritage themes

### Huichol Art
Uses intricate patterns and beaded textures from the Huichol indigenous culture. Features geometric and symbolic patterns, vibrant colors, and detailed repetitive motifs inspired by traditional beadwork.

**Best for:** Indigenous cultural representations, detailed pattern work, Jalisco/Nayarit region designs

### Oaxacan Wood Carving Style
Replicates the vibrant colors and detailed patterns of Oaxacan wood carvings (alebrijes). Features bright, contrasting colors and elaborate surface patterns on stylized figures.

**Best for:** Oaxaca designs, animal motifs, colorful cultural representations

### Amate Bark Painting Style
A simplified and colorful style, often depicting animals and nature, inspired by bark paintings. Features flat, bold shapes with vibrant colors and folk art naivety.

**Best for:** Nature-themed Mexican designs, indigenous art representations, Guerrero/Puebla regions

### Lotería Card Style
Simple, graphic style reminiscent of traditional Mexican bingo cards. Features bold outlines, flat colors, iconic simplified imagery, and vintage aesthetic.

**Best for:** Playful designs, retro Mexican themes, graphic representations of destinations

### Pre-Columbian Art (Modern Interpretation)
Uses the geometric and symbolic elements of ancient art with a contemporary twist. Features Aztec/Maya motifs, stepped pyramids, calendrical symbols, and earth-tone palettes updated with modern colors.

**Best for:** Archaeological sites, historical destinations, cultural heritage themes

### Talavera Pottery Style
Inspired by the intricate floral and animal patterns of traditional Talavera tiles. Features blue and white ceramic-inspired patterns, ornate florals, and decorative geometric borders.

**Best for:** Puebla designs, colonial architecture themes, decorative pattern work

## MODERN DIGITAL STYLES

### Cel-Shaded
Mimics traditional animation with flat colors and distinct shadows. Features hard-edged shadows, solid color fills, and cartoon-like rendering.

**Best for:** Playful designs, animation-inspired looks, bold graphic representations

### Simplified Graphic
Reduces complex objects to their most basic shapes. Features minimal detail, essential forms only, and clean geometric simplification.

**Best for:** Modern minimalist designs, icon-like representations, clean contemporary looks

### Iconographic
Uses simplified symbols to represent concepts or objects. Features universal visual language, high recognizability, and essential form communication.

**Best for:** Wayfinding designs, symbolic representations, modern graphic applications

### Geometric Style
Composed of clean, crisp shapes like circles, squares, and triangles. Features mathematical precision, sharp edges, and shape-based composition.

**Best for:** Modern architectural sites, abstract representations, contemporary designs

### Minimalism
Focuses on simplicity, using a limited color palette and simple shapes to create a clean and elegant design. Features negative space, restrained elements, and essential-only approach.

**Best for:** Modern cities, contemporary architecture, sophisticated representations
**Note:** Often conflicts with client preference for vibrant, dense designs

### Geometric Abstraction
Uses basic geometric shapes like circles, squares, and triangles to create abstract and symbolic designs. Features non-representational forms, symbolic meanings, and shape-based storytelling.

**Best for:** Abstract destination representations, modern art-inspired designs

### 3D/Isometric
Creates an illusion of depth and three-dimensionality, making designs pop and stand out. Features axonometric projection, layered depth, and dimensional appearance.

**Best for:** Architectural landmarks, cityscape designs, buildings and structures

### Flat Design
A minimalist design style that emphasizes usability. Features clean open space, crisp edges, bright colors, and two-dimensional illustrations.

**Best for:** Modern digital applications, clean graphic designs, tech-inspired looks
**Note:** May conflict with client preference for rich, dense compositions

### Vector
A popular style that uses points, lines, and curves to create sharp, clean graphics that can be scaled to any size without losing quality. Features crisp edges, smooth curves, and scalable artwork.

**Best for:** Logo-style designs, clean graphic representations, print-ready artwork

### Low Poly
A 3D modeling technique that uses a small number of polygons to create a faceted, abstract look. Features geometric facets, angular surfaces, and modern digital aesthetic.

**Best for:** Contemporary interpretations, abstract landscapes, modern tech-inspired designs

### Bento Box
A design trend that uses a grid of boxes to organize information in a visually appealing and easy-to-read way. Features organized compartments, clean separation, and structured layouts.

**Best for:** Multi-element designs, organized information displays, modern grid layouts

### Glassmorphism
A style that mimics the look of frosted glass, creating a sense of depth and transparency. Features translucent layers, blur effects, and light-inspired aesthetics.

**Best for:** Modern, elegant designs, layered compositions, contemporary digital looks

### Neo-Brutalism
Characterized by raw, unfinished concrete, bold, high-contrast visuals, and a minimalist approach. Features harsh contrasts, unapologetic boldness, and stark aesthetics.

**Best for:** Industrial sites, modern architecture, bold statement designs
**Note:** May conflict with client preference for colorful, playful designs

### Geominimalism
A sub-genre of minimalism that uses geometric shapes as the primary design element. Features shape-focused composition, clean lines, and restrained approach.

**Best for:** Modern abstract representations, architectural designs, contemporary looks
**Note:** May conflict with client preference for dense, decorative designs

## HISTORICAL ART MOVEMENTS

### Art Deco
A style from the 1920s and 30s known for its geometric shapes, rich colors, and luxurious feel. Features streamlined forms, metallic accents, geometric patterns, and elegant sophistication.

**Best for:** Historic hotels, 1920s-30s architecture, elegant vintage designs, grand buildings

### Art Nouveau
Characterized by its use of long, sinuous, organic lines and was used in architecture, interior design, jewelry and glass design, posters, and illustration. Features flowing curves, natural motifs, ornate details, and decorative elegance.

**Best for:** Belle Époque architecture, vintage travel posters, elegant historical buildings

### Pop Art
Inspired by popular culture, this style uses bright colors and bold imagery from advertising and comic books. Features Ben-Day dots, bold outlines, vibrant colors, and graphic simplicity.

**Best for:** Modern, playful designs, retro-inspired themes, bold graphic representations

### Surrealism
Features dreamlike, fantastical, and bizarre imagery that challenges logic and reason. Includes unexpected juxtapositions, dream-like scenarios, and imaginative compositions.

**Best for:** Fantasy destinations, creative interpretations, artistic abstract designs

### Cubism
An early-20th-century avant-garde art movement that revolutionized European painting and sculpture. Depicts subjects from multiple viewpoints simultaneously, featuring fragmented forms and geometric breakdown.

**Best for:** Artistic interpretations, abstract representations, modern art-inspired designs

### Impressionism
A 19th-century art movement characterized by relatively small, thin, yet visible brush strokes, open composition, and an emphasis on accurate depiction of light. Features soft edges, light emphasis, and atmospheric quality.

**Best for:** Landscape designs, soft romantic representations, artistic interpretations

### Expressionism
A modernist movement initially in poetry and painting, originating in Germany at the beginning of the 20th century. Presents the world solely from a subjective perspective, distorting it radically for emotional effect.

**Best for:** Emotional interpretations, dramatic representations, artistic mood pieces

### Bauhaus
A German art school operational from 1919 to 1933 that combined crafts and the fine arts. Features form follows function, geometric shapes, primary colors, and rational design.

**Best for:** Modern architecture, functional design, minimalist representations
**Note:** May conflict with client preference for ornate, decorative designs

### Constructivism
An early twentieth-century art movement founded in 1915. Features geometric abstraction, industrial materials aesthetic, bold diagonals, and functional art approach.

**Best for:** Industrial sites, modern abstract designs, bold geometric representations

### Dadaism
An art movement of the European avant-garde in the early 20th century. Features anti-art rebellion, nonsensical elements, collage techniques, and provocative compositions.

**Best for:** Creative experimental designs, artistic interpretations, unconventional approaches

## ILLUSTRATION & DRAWING STYLES

### Cartoon/Comic
A fun and playful style that uses exaggerated features and bright colors to create a lighthearted feel. Features bold outlines, simplified forms, expressive characters, and vibrant colors.

**Best for:** Playful destination designs, family-friendly themes, fun interpretations

### Doodle/Sketch
A loose, hand-drawn style that can be either simple and whimsical or detailed and intricate. Features spontaneous lines, casual execution, and hand-crafted feel.

**Best for:** Casual, approachable designs, whimsical interpretations, artistic sketchy looks

### Line Art
A style that uses only lines to create an image, without any shading or color. Features clean contours, no fill colors, and pure linear definition.

**Best for:** Minimalist designs, elegant simple representations, coloring book-style templates

### Caricature
An exaggerated or distorted representation of a person or thing, created for comic effect. Features emphasized characteristics, humorous distortion, and playful exaggeration.

**Best for:** Humorous designs, character-focused representations, playful interpretations

### Digital Painting (Simplified)
Looks like a painting but with fewer brushstrokes and flat colors. Features painterly aesthetic, simplified rendering, and digital execution.

**Best for:** Artistic yet accessible designs, painted looks with clarity

### Comic Book Style
Characterized by bold lines and vibrant, often unrealistic colors. Features action-oriented composition, dynamic poses, and graphic novel aesthetic.

**Best for:** Dynamic, energetic designs, bold graphic representations

### Vector Pop Art
A modern take on pop art using vector graphics and bright colors. Features crisp edges, bold colors, graphic simplicity, and contemporary execution.

**Best for:** Modern retro designs, bold graphic statements, vibrant representations

### Kawaii Illustration
A cute and cheerful style with exaggerated features and a simplified look. Features big eyes, round shapes, pastel or bright colors, and adorable character design.

**Best for:** Cute interpretations, child-friendly designs, playful kawaii aesthetic

### Chibi Art
Features characters with small bodies and oversized heads for a playful, youthful look. Includes super-deformed proportions, cute expressions, and simplified details.

**Best for:** Adorable mascot designs, playful character representations

### Retro Travel Poster
Mimics the style of vintage travel posters with bold text and stylized scenery. Features simplified landscapes, bold typography, limited color palettes, and nostalgic aesthetic.

**Best for:** Vintage destination designs, nostalgic representations, classic travel themes

### Children's Book Illustration
Whimsical and friendly with soft colors and simple, rounded shapes. Features approachable style, gentle palettes, and storytelling quality.

**Best for:** Family-friendly designs, gentle approachable representations

### Doodle Art
Appears hand-drawn with a free-flowing, spontaneous, and whimsical feel. Features casual execution, playful spontaneity, and artistic freedom.

**Best for:** Casual fun designs, hand-crafted aesthetic, playful representations

### Whimsical Cartoon
A style that is fantastical and playful, often with exaggerated proportions. Features imaginative elements, playful distortion, and dreamlike quality.

**Best for:** Fantasy-inspired designs, playful interpretations, magical themes

### Hand-Drawn Digital
Mimics the imperfections of hand-drawn art with a digital execution. Features organic irregularities, hand-crafted feel, and digital precision.

**Best for:** Artisanal designs, hand-crafted aesthetic, authentic imperfect beauty

### Vibrant Stylized Illustration
Characterized by its lively colors and non-realistic, stylized forms. Features bold color choices, artistic interpretation, and energetic execution.

**Best for:** Energetic designs, vibrant representations, artistic interpretations

### Abstract Pop
Combines abstract elements with a pop art sensibility and bright colors. Features non-representational forms, bold colors, and graphic impact.

**Best for:** Modern artistic designs, abstract representations, bold statements

### Psychedelic Cartoon
Uses surreal, swirling patterns and bold, contrasting colors for a vibrant effect. Features distorted perspectives, vivid colors, and dreamlike quality.

**Best for:** Retro-inspired designs, festival themes, vibrant surreal representations

## TRADITIONAL ART MEDIA STYLES

### Watercolor
A painting method in which the paints are made of pigments suspended in a water-based solution. Features soft edges, translucent layers, organic bleeds, and dreamy quality.

**Best for:** Soft romantic designs, artistic interpretations, gentle landscapes

### Charcoal
A dry art medium made of finely ground organic materials that are held together by a gum or wax binder. Features soft gradations, smudgy effects, dramatic contrast, and expressive marks.

**Best for:** Dramatic artistic designs, moody representations, expressive interpretations

### Pen and Ink
A drawing technique that uses black and other colored inks which are applied to a surface with a pen or brush. Features precise lines, controlled marks, crosshatching, and graphic clarity.

**Best for:** Detailed illustrations, classic drawing aesthetics, precise representations

### Acrylic
A fast-drying paint made of pigment suspended in acrylic polymer emulsion. Features bold colors, painterly texture, versatile effects, and vibrant finish.

**Best for:** Bold painted designs, colorful artistic representations, expressive paintings

### Engraving
The practice of incising a design onto a hard, usually flat surface by cutting grooves into it with a burin. Features fine lines, crosshatching, detailed rendering, and classical aesthetic.

**Best for:** Historic buildings, classical representations, detailed vintage looks
**Note:** Often creates heavy black tones - conflicts with client preference

### Woodcut
A relief printing technique in which an image is carved into the surface of a block of wood. Features bold lines, stark contrast, textured appearance, and handcrafted quality.

**Best for:** Bold graphic designs, folk art aesthetics, traditional craft looks

## PRINT & TEXTURE STYLES

### Paper Cutout Style
Mimics the look of layered, cut paper with hard edges and distinct shadows. Features layered depth, clean edges, dimensional illusion, and crafted appearance.

**Best for:** Layered collage designs, dimensional representations, crafted aesthetics

### Risograph Print
Characterized by its grainy textures, imperfect alignment, and vibrant, overlapping colors. Features printing imperfections, limited color layers, and vintage reproduction quality.

**Best for:** Retro designs, alternative aesthetics, vintage print looks

### Screen Print Style
Reproduces the look of screen-printed art with solid colors and bold lines. Features flat colors, slight registration variations, and handmade print quality.

**Best for:** Bold graphic posters, vintage print aesthetics, limited color designs

### Sticker Style
Looks like a die-cut sticker with a clean white border and glossy finish. Features white outline, irregular cutout shape, and vinyl sticker appearance.

**Best for:** Modern souvenir designs, die-cut products, contemporary graphic looks

### Textured Flat Illustration
Combines a flat design with subtle, grainy textures for added depth. Features digital grain, subtle noise, and tactile quality within flat aesthetic.

**Best for:** Modern designs with depth, textured contemporary looks

### Collage Style (Digital)
Uses separate graphic elements combined into a cohesive piece. Features mixed media appearance, layered elements, varied textures, and assembled composition.

**Best for:** Mixed-element designs, eclectic representations, assembled aesthetics

### Monochromatic with Pop of Color
Uses a single color palette with one or two contrasting, vibrant colors. Features restrained palette, strategic accent color, and focused visual impact.

**Best for:** Sophisticated designs, focused attention, elegant color strategies

### Felt Appliqué Style
Mimics the look of layered felt fabric with soft edges and flat colors. Features crafted textile appearance, layered depth, and handmade quality.

**Best for:** Soft crafted designs, textile-inspired aesthetics, gentle layered looks

### Embroidered Look
Features a textured, stitched look with thick lines and vibrant colors. Includes thread-like lines, textile texture, and handcrafted embroidery appearance.

**Best for:** Textile-inspired designs, crafted aesthetics, decorative looks

### Stencil Art
Uses solid shapes and clear separations, as if created with stencils. Features hard edges, simplified forms, spray paint aesthetic, and urban graphic quality.

**Best for:** Urban designs, street art aesthetics, bold graphic looks

## RETRO & VINTAGE STYLES

### Retro/Vintage
Inspired by the design styles of the past, from the 1950s, 60s, 70s, 80s, and 90s. Features period-specific aesthetics, nostalgic color palettes, and era-appropriate typography.

**Best for:** Nostalgic designs, period-specific representations, vintage travel themes

### Psychedelic
Characterized by bright, swirling colors and distorted visuals, inspired by the 1960s counterculture. Features vibrating colors, warped perspectives, and mind-bending patterns.

**Best for:** 1960s-inspired designs, festival themes, vibrant surreal representations

### Grunge
A style inspired by the 1990s music scene, characterized by a dirty, distressed, and messy aesthetic. Features rough textures, distressed elements, and rebellious attitude.

**Best for:** Urban edgy designs, alternative aesthetics, rough textured looks
**Note:** May conflict with client preference for clean, vibrant designs

### Y2K Evolution
A style inspired by the late 1990s and early 2000s, featuring futuristic and tech-inspired elements. Includes chrome effects, digital aesthetics, and millennium-era optimism.

**Best for:** Retro-futuristic designs, tech-inspired themes, nostalgic modern looks

### American Kitsch
A style that is considered to be in poor taste but is appreciated in an ironic or knowing way. Features over-the-top elements, ironic nostalgia, and playful bad taste.

**Best for:** Ironic designs, playful retro themes, tongue-in-cheek representations

### Punk
Inspired by the 1970s punk rock scene, this style is characterized by a rebellious, anti-establishment attitude. Features DIY aesthetic, bold typography, aggressive visuals, and anarchic spirit.

**Best for:** Edgy urban designs, rebellious themes, alternative aesthetics

## CULTURAL & ETHNIC STYLES

### Folk Art
Art originating from a specific culture, typically with a naive or traditional style. Features handcrafted quality, cultural authenticity, traditional motifs, and regional character.

**Best for:** Cultural destinations, traditional town designs, authentic regional representations

### Tribal
Inspired by the art and culture of indigenous peoples from around the world. Features symbolic patterns, traditional motifs, cultural significance, and ancestral aesthetics.

**Best for:** Indigenous cultural sites, traditional pattern work, cultural heritage themes

### Ukiyo-e
A genre of Japanese art which flourished from the 17th through 19th centuries. Features woodblock print aesthetic, flowing lines, flat colors, and Japanese composition principles.

**Best for:** Japanese-inspired designs, historical aesthetics, elegant linear compositions

### Ethnic Patterns
Designs that are inspired by the traditional patterns and motifs of a particular culture or ethnic group. Features cultural authenticity, traditional repetition, and symbolic meanings.

**Best for:** Cultural destination designs, pattern-based compositions, heritage themes

## CONCEPTUAL & ABSTRACT STYLES

### Fantasy
A genre of speculative fiction set in a fictional universe, often inspired by real world myth and folklore. Features magical elements, mythical creatures, imaginative worlds, and fantastical scenarios.

**Best for:** Imaginative interpretations, magical themes, creative fantasy designs

### Nature-Inspired
Designs that feature elements from nature, such as flowers, leaves, animals, and landscapes. Features organic forms, botanical elements, natural color palettes, and environmental themes.

**Best for:** Natural destinations, eco-tourism themes, organic landscape designs

### Typography
The art and technique of arranging type to make written language legible, readable, and appealing when displayed. Features text as primary element, letterform focus, and typographic hierarchy.

**Best for:** Text-heavy designs, destination name emphasis, typographic posters

### Photorealism
A genre of art that encompasses painting, drawing and other graphic media, in which an artist studies a photograph and then attempts to reproduce the image as realistically as possible. Features photo-accurate detail, realistic rendering, and precise representation.

**Best for:** Realistic landmark representations, detailed architectural designs

### Abstract
Art that does not attempt to represent an accurate depiction of a visual reality but instead uses shapes, colors, forms and gestural marks to achieve its effect. Features non-representational forms, expressive color, and conceptual interpretation.

**Best for:** Artistic interpretations, modern abstract designs, conceptual representations

### Collage
A work of art made from an assemblage of different forms, thus creating a new whole. Features mixed elements, layered composition, varied textures, and assembled aesthetic.

**Best for:** Mixed-media designs, eclectic representations, layered storytelling

## DIGITAL & CONTEMPORARY STYLES

### Pixel Art
A form of digital art, created through the use of software, where images are edited on the pixel level. Features blocky aesthetic, retro video game look, limited resolution, and nostalgic digital quality.

**Best for:** Retro gaming-inspired designs, 8-bit aesthetics, digital nostalgia themes

### Glitch Art
The practice of using digital or analog errors for aesthetic purposes by either corrupting digital data or physically manipulating electronic devices. Features digital artifacts, corrupted visuals, and technological errors as art.

**Best for:** Contemporary digital designs, tech-inspired themes, modern experimental looks

### Graffiti/Street Art
Visual art created in public locations, usually unsanctioned artwork executed outside of the context of traditional art venues. Features spray paint aesthetic, urban character, bold letters, and street culture.

**Best for:** Urban destinations, street culture themes, contemporary city designs

### Cyberpunk
A subgenre of science fiction in a futuristic setting that tends to focus on a "combination of low-life and high tech". Features neon colors, dystopian themes, technological elements, and futuristic urban aesthetics.

**Best for:** Futuristic city designs, tech-inspired themes, modern sci-fi interpretations

### Line Art (Modern)
Simple, clean lines that define a subject without shading or color. Features minimalist approach, essential contours only, and contemporary linear aesthetic.

**Best for:** Modern minimalist designs, clean graphic looks, contemporary line work

### Minimalist Illustration
Uses a limited number of lines and shapes to create an impactful image. Features essential elements only, negative space, and restrained composition.

**Best for:** Modern sophisticated designs, clean contemporary looks
**Note:** Often conflicts with client preference for rich, dense designs

### Simple Iconography
Reduces elements to their most basic forms for universal recognition. Features symbolic simplification, instant recognition, and essential form communication.

**Best for:** Modern graphic designs, symbolic representations, universal visual language

### Graphic Design (Conceptual)
Focuses on conveying a single idea through bold shapes and colors. Features concept-driven composition, clear message, and impactful visual communication.

**Best for:** Message-focused designs, conceptual representations, idea-driven graphics

### Corporate Memphis (Simplified)
Uses a specific style of geometric human figures and bright, flat colors. Features abstract human forms, flat colors, geometric limbs, and contemporary corporate aesthetic.

**Best for:** Modern friendly designs, contemporary accessible looks
**Note:** May feel too corporate for souvenir applications

### Brand Mascot Style
A friendly, engaging, and simplified character design style. Features approachable characters, simplified forms, and brand-friendly aesthetics.

**Best for:** Character-based designs, mascot representations, friendly branding

### Illustrative Logo Style
Focuses on creating a memorable image that can function as a logo. Features iconic simplicity, memorable forms, and scalable design.

**Best for:** Logo-style souvenirs, iconic representations, brand-focused designs

### Bold Typography Integration
The text itself is a major, graphical part of the design. Features text as design element, typographic dominance, and letterform artistry.

**Best for:** Type-focused designs, destination name emphasis, typographic posters

### Infographic Style
Visually represents information through clear, stylized icons and graphics. Features data visualization, icon-based communication, and information design principles.

**Best for:** Information-rich designs, educational graphics, multi-fact representations

### Travel Motif Style
Uses recurring, symbolic elements related to travel and destinations. Features travel iconography, destination symbols, and tourism-related visual language.

**Best for:** Classic souvenir designs, travel-themed representations, tourism graphics

## STYLE SELECTION GUIDELINES

### ✅ CLIENT-APPROVED STYLES (Use Frequently):
- **Mexican Folk Art** - Vibrant, cultural, decorative
- **Papel Picado Style** - Festive, intricate, colorful
- **Alebrije Style** - Whimsical, vibrant, fantastical
- **Day of the Dead** - Celebratory, colorful, cultural
- **Collage** - Mixed media, layered, eclectic
- **Vibrant Stylized Illustration** - Energetic, colorful, dynamic
- **Cartoon/Comic** - Playful, bold, fun
- **Retro Travel Poster** - Nostalgic, bold, classic

### ⚠️ USE WITH CAUTION (May Conflict with Client Preferences):
- **Minimalism** - Too sparse, not vibrant enough
- **Flat Design** - Can be too minimal
- **Bauhaus** - Too geometric and restrained
- **Engraving** - Too much black, heavy shadows
- **Neo-Brutalism** - Too harsh, lacks color
- **Grunge** - Can be too messy/dark

### ❌ AVOID FOR SOUVENIR DESIGNS:
- Styles with heavy black tones or shadows (conflicts with client preference)
- Overly symmetric or rigid compositions (clients prefer dynamic, irregular)
- Minimal/sparse styles (clients prefer rich, dense compositions)
- Muted or desaturated color schemes (clients prefer vibrant, saturated colors)

## USAGE IN PROMPTS

When specifying a style in a design prompt, use this format:

**Style: [Style Name]**

Then include the key characteristics from this reference document to guide the AI accurately. For example:

**Style: Mexican Folk Art**
- Bold, vibrant colors throughout
- Intricate traditional patterns and motifs
- Cultural symbolism from Mexican regions
- Handcrafted decorative quality
- Rich, saturated color palette

---

**Note:** Always combine style choices with the mandatory design requirements:
- Super colorful (10+ vibrant colors)
- Irregular organic outline (NO rectangular frames)
- Elements interacting dynamically
- Dense, rich composition (8/10 saturation typical)
- NO black tones or heavy shadows
- State name included (for Mexican destinations)
- Pueblos Mágicos branding (if applicable)
- "MÉXICO" official logo letters
