## AGENT 2: CONTEXT BUILDING

### Location: Castillo de Chapultepec, Ciudad de México (Mexico City)

**Historic Context**:
- Built 1785-1787 on Chapultepec Hill
- Former imperial palace (Emperor Maximilian I)
- Presidential residence (1884-1939)
- Now National Museum of History
- Only castle in North America to house sovereigns
- Symbol of Mexican history and independence

**Geographic Setting**:
- Located in Bosque de Chapultepec (Chapultepec Park)
- On top of Chapultepec Hill
- Overlooks Paseo de la Reforma
- In heart of Mexico City
- Surrounded by ancient ahuehuete trees (Montezuma cypress)

**Cultural Significance**:
- National Historic Monument
- Symbol of Mexican sovereignty
- Museum housing important historical artifacts
- Tourist destination
- Educational center
- Represents Mexican independence and history

**Visual Character of Mexico City Culture**:
- Vibrant colors (inspired by muralism, folk art)
- Rich indigenous heritage (Mexica/Aztec)
- Colonial architecture
- Modern cosmopolitan city
- Cultural diversity
- Art, music, food traditions
